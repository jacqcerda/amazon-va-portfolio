## What This Is

This is a fully mocked-up Amazon product listing built to demonstrate end-to-end listing optimization skills: keyword research, title/bullet copywriting, image briefing, A+ Content planning, and listing audit methodology.

**"Loopa" is an invented brand** — not a real product or seller. The optimization approach is based on real competitor research in the silicone sensory toy niche (weak listings, common buyer complaints, gaps in existing copy), fully anonymized: no ASINs, no competitor brand names, no verbatim review quotes, no screenshots of real listings.

## The Problem (Before)

Research across comparable listings in this niche surfaced a consistent pattern:

- **Grip/size complaints** — a recurring buyer pain point (pieces too small for little hands) went unaddressed in existing listing copy
- **Weak bullet structure** — feature-dumping without benefit framing, no scannable hooks
- **Thin or missing description content** — bullets simply repeated rather than expanded on
- **Unclear keyword strategy** — titles either keyword-stuffed or missing the primary search term entirely

Full scorecard audit and problem-by-problem breakdown are covered in the methodology section below.

## The Fix (After)

**Title, bullets, and A+ Content rewritten** around a validated keyword set (primary, secondary, and long-tail terms placed per Amazon's indexing logic — each keyword appearing once, backend reserved for synonyms and unused variants).

**Bullet 2 was written specifically to close the grip/size gap** identified in research — a direct example of using buyer language and review-mining insight to drive a copy decision, not just filling in a template.

**Full 8-image carousel + 3 A+ Content modules** planned and iteratively refined through a manual AI-image QA process — see below.

## Methodology Note: AI Image QA Process

Every image in this set went through multiple rounds of visual consistency review against a **locked visual identity spec** (board silhouette, texture panel shapes, string count/color, star piece detailing). Recurring issues caught and corrected across iterations included:

- Board silhouette drifting from rectangular to square/diamond
- Texture panels rendering as quarter-circle wedges instead of straight-edged rectangles
- String count reading as 2 instead of 4 due to AI defaulting to a "threading/lacing" interpretation
- Star piece color and open-center-hole inconsistencies across shots
- Left/right consistency checks on a split-frame lifestyle image

This process mirrors the same QA discipline used in a real Amazon catalog audit: comparing every visual/copy element against a spec, flagging deviations, and deciding what's a genuine compliance issue versus an acceptable trade-off — rather than accepting AI or vendor output uncritically.
